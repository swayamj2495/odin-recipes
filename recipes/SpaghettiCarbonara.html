Spaghetti Carbonara

Description :-

Spaghetti Carbonara is a classic Roman pasta dish made with eggs, cheese, cured pork, and black pepper. It is known for its rich, creamy sauce that comes from emulsifying raw egg yolks with hot pasta, rather than using cream.

The traditional recipe uses guanciale (cured pork cheek), Pecorino Romano cheese, and plenty of black pepper, though pancetta or bacon can be substituted. This dish is quick, simple, and deeply flavorful.

Ingredients :-

For the Pasta

  • Spaghetti (or bucatini)
  • Salt (for pasta water)

For the Sauce

  • Guanciale (or pancetta/bacon)
  • Egg yolks
  • Pecorino Romano cheese (grated)
  • Black pepper (freshly cracked)

Recipe :-

  1. Cook Pasta
    • Boil spaghetti in salted water until al dente.
    • Reserve 1 cup of pasta water, then drain.
  2. Cook the Meat
    • In a skillet, saut√© guanciale until crispy.
  3. Make the Sauce
    • In a bowl, whisk egg yolks with grated Pecorino and pepper.
  4. Combine
    • Add hot pasta to skillet with guanciale.
    • Remove from heat, then quickly mix in egg mixture.
    • Add reserved pasta water gradually until creamy.
  5. Serve
    • Plate immediately with extra Pecorino and pepper on top.
