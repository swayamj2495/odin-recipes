Lasagna

Description :-

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made by layering wide, flat pasta sheets with rich fillings such as meat sauce, cheese, and béchamel. Traditionally, it includes a savory ragù (often made with ground beef, pork, or sausage simmered in tomato sauce and herbs), ricotta or béchamel for creaminess, and plenty of melted mozzarella and Parmesan for flavor. The layers are stacked in a baking dish and baked until bubbly, golden, and slightly crispy on top.

There are many variations worldwide: some use vegetables like spinach, zucchini, or eggplant in place of meat, while others feature seafood or even purely cheese-based fillings. In Italy, different regions have their own takes—for example, Lasagna alla Bolognese uses slow-cooked ragù with béchamel, while southern versions might rely more heavily on ricotta and tomato sauce.

Lasagna is often served as a hearty main course, especially at family gatherings, holidays, and celebrations. It’s beloved for its comforting balance of flavors, satisfying texture, and ability to feed a crowd.

Ingredients :-

For the Meat Sauce (Ragu)

  • Olive oil (for sauteing)
  • Onion (finely chopped)
  • Garlic (minced)
  • Ground Beef (or a mix of beef and pork/sausage)
  • Tomato Paste
  • Crushed Tomatoes (or tomato sauce)
  • Fresh or dired herbs(basil, oregano, thyme, bay leaf)
  • Salt and black pepper
  • Red pepper flakes (optional, for a little heat)
  • Red wine (optional, for depth of flavor)

For the Cheese Layer

  • Ricotta cheese (or cottage cheese, depending on preference)
  • Egg (to help bind the ricotta mixture)
  • Fresh parsley (chopped)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese

For the Pasta & Topping

  • Lasagna noodles (regular or no-boil)
  • Mozzarella cheese (shredded)
  • Extra Parmesan (for topping)

Optional Additions/ Variations

  • Béchamel sauce (used in traditional Italian recipes instead of ricotta)
  • Spinach or other vegetables (for a veggie lasagna)
  • Mushrooms, zucchini, or eggplant (for extra texture and flavor)

Recipe:-

  1. Make the Heat Sauce
    • Heat olive oil in a large skillet or pot.
    • Sauté onion until soft, then add garlic for 1 minute.
    • Add ground beef, cooking until browned.
    • Stir in tomato paste, then add crushed tomatoes, wine (if using), oregano, basil, sugar, salt, and pepper.
    • Simmer gently for 25–30 minutes (longer if possible for deeper flavor).
  2. Prepare the Cheese Mixture
    • In a bowl, mix ricotta, egg, Parmesan, and parsley until smooth.
  3. Cook the Lasagna Noodles (if not using no-boil)
    • Boil noodles in salted water until al dente. Drain and lay flat on parchment paper to prevent sticking.
  4. Assemble the Lasagna
    • Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
    • In a baking dish (9x13 inch / 23x33 cm), spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom.
    • Layer as follows:
      1. Noodles
      2. Meat Sauce
      3. Ricotta Mixture
      4. Mozzarella
    • Repeat layers until ingredients are used up, finishing with sauce and mozzarella + Parmesan on top.
  5. Bake
    • Cover with foil (to prevent burning) and bake for 25 minutes.
    • Remove foil and bake another 20 minutes until golden and bubbly.
    • Let rest for 10–15 minutes before slicing (this helps it set).
